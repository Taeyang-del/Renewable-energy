    Our Services

    • What is renewable energy

      • Renewable energy is eco-friendly because it produces minimal carbon dioxide and harmful greenhouse gases. It also benefits the environment, as 99 percent of the Earth’s population requires clean oxygen, which renewable energy helps provide. Other advantages of renewable energy include health benefits, job creation, economic growth, affordability, and abundant availability. Renewable energy types encompass solar, wind, geothermal, biomass, and hydropower.

    • Solar Energy

      • Solar energy operates by utilizing photovoltaic and photoelectrochemical cells to absorb and convert sunlight into renewable electricity. Solar panels employ nitrides to enhance the absorption of solar energy, ensuring improved electrical efficiency. Moreover, nitrides possess unexplored material properties that may be beneficial in the future. Solar panels comprise various chemicals and crystal structures.

    • Biomass Energy

      • What is biomass? You're in luck because I’m about to explain! Biomass energy is renewable and one of the most utilized energy sources globally. It is frequently used for cooking, heating, and much more. Sources of biomass energy include firewood, wood pellets, wood chips, lumber, furniture mill sawdust, and black liquor from pulp and paper mills. It also includes crops, biogenic materials, animal manure, human sewage, and more. Biomass can be converted into energy in several ways, including direct combustion, thermochemical conversion, chemical conversion, and biological conversion. The most common method of converting biomass into energy is direct combustion. When biomass is used to produce energy, it can be burned to heat buildings and water, generate energy, and produce electricity in steam turbines.

    • Wind Energy

      • As you know, wind turbines are a type of renewable energy. But do you know how they operate? A wind turbine converts wind energy into electricity to power various applications, such as homes and communities. When the wind blows against the blades, the air pressure decreases slightly. Then, the pressure difference across the two sides of the blade creates drag and lift. After that, the force of the lift is stronger than the drag, which causes the rotor to spin. Since the rotor is connected to the generator, either directly or through a shaft and a series of gears, this speeds up the rotation and allows a physical generator to create energy. There are transmission lines that carry electricity at high voltages. The transformers receive AC electricity at a voltage that is increased or decreased to deliver the electricity as needed.

    • Hydropower

      • Humans have been harnessing or attempting to utilize hydroelectric energy for use. Today, harnessing the movement of water is known as hydroelectric energy. Even though hydropower doesn't emit pollution or greenhouse gases, it can have negative environmental and social consequences. For example, blocking rivers with dams can degrade water quality, damage aquatic and riparian habitats, block migratory fish passages, and displace local communities. As you know, water is constantly moved around in different states. However, if we use hydroelectric energy responsibly, it could be a sustainable and nonpolluting source of electricity. This could also reduce our dependence on fossil fuels and lessen a significant threat to global warming. In 2011, hydroelectric power provided 16 percent of the world’s electricity, second to fossil fuels. This had a considerable impact on the world a long time ago. China contributed 24 percent, the United States eight percent, and Brazil nine percent.

    • Geothermal Energy

      • Geothermal energy harnesses heat primarily from the ground or areas that absorb substantial heat from the sun. If we don’t transition to renewable energy like geothermal, fossil fuels may be depleted within about 50 years. So, what can we do? We can utilize energy sources that do not rely on fossil fuels! These include geothermal, solar, biomass, wind, and hydropower. Today, I will focus on geothermal energy. While various forms of energy are labeled as “renewable,” not all are sustainable or environmentally friendly. Many regions worldwide view geothermal energy as an affordable and sustainable solution, potentially reducing dependence on fossil fuels, combating global warming, and mitigating public health risks associated with fossil fuels. Geothermal energy facilities can generate 68 billion kilowatt-hours of electricity and are increasingly adopted worldwide, with more than 25% of Iceland and El Salvador's electricity produced from geothermal sources.
